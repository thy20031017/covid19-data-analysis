# RECOMMENDED DATA EXPLORATION QUESTIONS FOR UM_C19_2021.csv

## DATASET OVERVIEW
This is a University of Michigan COVID-19 testing dataset from August 16, 2020 to August 15, 2021 (365 days), containing:
- 1063 data entries
- 190,188 total tests conducted
- 3,495 positive cases (1.84% overall positivity rate)
- Data split between Students (705 entries) and Faculty/Staff (358 entries)
- Residence information: Non-Residential, Students - Non-Residential, Students - Residential

## KEY EXPLORATION QUESTIONS

### 1. TEMPORAL PATTERNS & TRENDS
- How did the positivity rate change over the academic year?
- Were there any notable peaks in positive cases? When and why might they have occurred?
- How did testing volume fluctuate throughout the year?
- Were there differences in testing patterns between academic semesters?
- Did the ratio of positive to negative tests change significantly month-to-month?
- Were weekends and holidays associated with different testing patterns or positivity rates?

### 2. DEMOGRAPHIC COMPARISONS (STUDENTS VS FACULTY/STAFF)
- What was the overall positivity rate difference between students and faculty/staff?
- Did one group consistently have higher positivity rates than the other?
- How did testing volumes compare between students and faculty/staff?
- Did students show more volatility in case numbers than faculty/staff?
- Were there any notable events where one group had a sudden increase in positive tests?

### 3. RESIDENCE-BASED ANALYSIS
- Did residential students have different positivity rates compared to non-residential students?
- How did on-campus living affect the spread of COVID-19?
- Were there specific periods where residential cases spiked?
- Did testing volumes differ significantly between residential and non-residential groups?
- How did the ratio of tests per person compare between different residence types?

### 4. RISK ASSESSMENT & PUBLIC HEALTH IMPLICATIONS
- What percentage of the university population appears to have been tested regularly?
- How effective were the university's testing protocols in detecting cases?
- Were there periods when testing capacity may have been insufficient?
- What trends might have informed university decisions about campus operations?
- How did the university's positivity rates compare to local community rates?

### 5. COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS
- How do weekly testing patterns compare across different months?
- Which demographic/residence group contributed most to total positive cases?
- Were there any groups that showed consistently lower/higher positivity rates?
- How did the testing strategy evolve for different groups throughout the year?
- What was the relative contribution of each group to the overall testing volume?

### 6. DATA QUALITY & METHODOLOGY CONSIDERATIONS
- Were testing frequencies consistent across different groups?
- Is there evidence of changes in testing strategies throughout the year?
- Were there any days with unusually high or low testing volumes that might warrant investigation?
- Are there any anomalies in the data that should be verified?

### 7. PUBLIC POLICY & INTERVENTION ANALYSIS
- Can you correlate any spikes in cases with specific university events or policy changes?
- How did testing rates change in response to potential policy shifts?
- Were there periods when testing appeared to increase proactively?
- What does the data suggest about the effectiveness of different testing strategies?
